💡 Core Concept Step 02 00:02:30 → 00:04:20▶ Watch this step

Build the tight half guard frame

  • Trap the far foot over the top player's foot and pull it in tight.
  • Keep the elbow in front of the knee so the knees can't be smashed together.
  • Block the top player's hand from reaching your head.

The frame is what makes the top player's job difficult before any sweep happens: with the foot trapped and the elbow blocking the knees, they can't just turn the legs out and pass, and with the head blocked they have no easy grip to add pressure with. Every option that follows depends on holding this structure first.

Bait the pass and kick the leg free

  • Frame, frame, frame - grab the top player's wrist as they reach through the leg weave.
  • Track and pull the trapped foot in, then lift the toe as they try to kick it back.
  • Forward shrimp, ankle the foot out, and kick the leg straight up in the air.

Letting the top player commit to the leg-weave pass is the bait - once they've leaned in to force it, lifting the toe and kicking the leg straight up turns their own pressure against them. The forward shrimp right after is what carries the hips through into the finish instead of just resetting.

Grip outside the leg, not underneath
